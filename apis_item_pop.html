  • [145376] Red Iron Oxide Cathaycoat RA11A – Distributor Change & Implications
    Last Updated by : Brendan O'Keefe
    Last Updated On : 25-Jun-2025
    editEdit Post
    deleteDelete Post
    Background
    Red Iron Oxide Cathaycoat RA11A (Material #145376) has been widely used as a pigment in our water-based architectural coatings. It offers excellent dispersibility, color stability, and compatibility with our emulsion systems.

    Issue Summary
    As of September 2023, our procurement team reported a change in the distributor for RA11A due to regional restructuring by the manufacturer, Cathay Industries. Initial batches from the new distributor (DeltaChem Korea) raised concerns related to:
    Slight increase in median particle size (D50 ~0.45 μm vs. spec 0.3–0.4 μm)
    Increased batch-to-batch color variation (∆E > 1.0 in some cases)
    Reduced performance in gloss retention during accelerated weathering tests

    Investigation
    QC and Application Lab jointly conducted revalidation tests. XRD and SEM-EDX results indicate no change in crystal phase or impurity levels. However, the dispersion stability in acrylic binder showed slightly higher viscosity rise over 1 week (Brookfield @ 25°C: +8%).

    Action Taken
    Feedback shared with new distributor; tighter particle size control requested.
    Temporary hold placed on large-scale reformulation using RA11A until spec deviation is resolved.
    Alternative pigment grades (e.g., RA11B, Bayer 130M) are under screening for possible substitution.

    Recommendations
    Continue using RA11A for low-performance grades under internal-use-only label.
    For exterior or high-gloss applications, proceed only after additional QC confirmation.
    Cross-functional alignment between procurement, lab, and QA is essential for ongoing monitoring.

  • Surface Defects in Silicone Resin Coatings: Root Causes & Mitigation Strategies
    Last Updated by : Joe Zampella
    Last Updated On : 05-Jun-2025
    Background
    During recent application testing of our methyl silicone resin system (SR-880 series), several surface defects—including cratering, pinholes, and fisheyes—were observed on coated aluminum substrates. These defects were inconsistent across batches and appeared more prominently after viscosity adjustments.

    Root Cause Analysis
    Cratering: Identified as caused by localized contamination (likely surfactant residue) or poor substrate wetting.
    Pinholes: Correlated with fast solvent evaporation during spray application. Environmental RH < 35% was a contributing factor.
    Fisheyes: Traced back to cross-contamination from incompatible silicone fluids in shared equipment.

    Corrective Measures
    Implemented stricter solvent filtration and substrate pre-cleaning (IPA wipe + baked drying).
    Modified formulation to include 0.1% flow agent (Silwet L-7500) to improve leveling.
    Added flash-off delay before thermal cure step to reduce entrapped volatiles.

    Results
    Post-implementation, surface smoothness improved by over 85% (visual inspection and 3D surface scan), and defect rate dropped below 1%. Internal spec alignment has been updated accordingly.
