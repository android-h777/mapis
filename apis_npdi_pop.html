Definitions for determining the Magnitude of the Impact
close
LowBenefit (score +1) ModerateBenefit (score +3) SignificantBenefit (Score +9) NegativeImpact (score -3)
The PAC has reduced use of hazardous chemicals in the production or has reduced tox profile than earlier internal reference The PAC has reduced use of hazardous chemicals in the production or has reduced tox profile than earlier external reference Does not contain prohibited/regulated chemicals (Cat 1) and help in reducing the tox profile of the customer's product The PAC has increased use of hazardous chemicals in the production or has increased tox profile than earlier internal reference
Interpretation of the categorical and Numerical scores
close
If the product is showing moderate to significant impacts in even one signal in any of the sub categories, then the categorial outcome is A. i.e Product with sustainability improvements or Beneficial . However, if there is any response which is NEGATIVE, then, the category of the NPI moves to C. If there are only low impact on the benefits, then the NPI is marked as Category B or sustainability Neutral NPI.
The value of the numerical score indicates the strength of the product's sustainability benefits collectively. It is an internal indicator to distinguish products which are highly aligned to sustainability criteria positively
SUSTAINABILITY CATEGORY IN NPDI
Beneficial Impact A No negative impacts and some moderate or significant benefits
Neutral B No negative impacts but only low benefits
Negative Impact C if any negative impacts
